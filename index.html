About Me

I'm a graduate of the University of Michigan (B.Mus) and University of Ottawa (M.Mus) schools of music, currently freelancing in Ontario and the Midwest USA.

I'm available for lessons or short term performance engagements depending on location and keep a stock of ensemble repertoire for background music engagements.

I've performed with the Ottawa Symphony, Thunder Bay Symphony, Strings of St. John's (Ottawa), Arbor Opera Theatre Orchestra, and the UM Gilbert & Sullivan Society Orchestra. I also have been fortunate to have performed in dozens of conducting workshops and recitals while at Michigan, including for Elim Chan, Yaniv Segal, Yaniv Dinur, Chris Whittaker, Matthew B. Dell, Warren Puffer Jones, and Oriol Sans. As a professional I've worked under the batons of David Currie, Alain Treudel, Arthur Post, Nadege Foofat, Jean-Michel Malouf, Rei Hotoda, Warren Puffer Jones and others.

In small ensembles I've performed many times for weddings and other events in Ottawa, as well as recording with the Cumberland Quartet for the 2017 Moonfruits album Ste. Quequeparte, and the Canadian Poet Brandon Wint.

Other Hobbies

I've been lucky to get invited to play in lots of unexpected places and have excuses to travel, and do whatever the more natural version of "street photography" there is.

This orange floof is my cat. I got him from my violin teacher in Washington State sixteen years ago and promptly renamed him from "Pumpkin" to "Fiddle", in honor of a cat in Diana Wynne Jones' Chrestomanci universe.
